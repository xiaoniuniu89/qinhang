- **Performance** - Playing prepared pieces

## Which Exam Board Should You Choose?

### ABRSM
- Best for: International recognition, studying abroad, comprehensive curriculum
- Widely accepted worldwide
- Structured, traditional approach
- **College entrance**: Commonly required for music college admission (typically Grade 8 + Theory)

### RIAM
- Best for: Irish students, integration with Irish school system
- National recognition in Ireland
- Irish repertoire included
- **College entrance**: Often required for Irish third-level music programs (typically Grade 8 + Theory)

### School Exams (Junior/Leaving Cert)
- Required for: Irish secondary school students taking music as a subject
- Contributes to overall school results and CAO points
- Important for college applications
- Performance component is a significant part of the final grade

### Why RIAM and ABRSM Are Important in Ireland

Both RIAM and ABRSM examinations are highly valued in Ireland, particularly for students considering music at third level (college/university).

**College Entrance Requirements:**
- Most Irish music colleges and university music programs require **Grade 8** in piano (RIAM or ABRSM)
- **Music theory** qualifications are also typically required (Grade 5 theory or equivalent)
- These qualifications demonstrate serious commitment and advanced proficiency
- Both exam boards are equally recognized by Irish third-level institutions

**Even if you're not pursuing music as a career**, achieving Grade 8 demonstrates:
- Dedication and perseverance
- High-level skill development
- Academic achievement that looks excellent on CVs and college applications

**CC can help you decide** which exam board is most suitable for your goals and circumstances. Many students pursue multiple examination systems simultaneously.

- **Beginners**: Generally start formal exams after 1-2 years of foundational learning
- **Preparation period**: Typically 6-12 months for each grade level
- **Timing**: Exams usually held twice per year (summer and winter sessions)
